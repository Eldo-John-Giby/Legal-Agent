=== VERDICT ===
The Plaintiff, Acme Corp., is awarded $40,000 in damages.

=== FINDINGS OF FACT ===
- The Defendant, Jordan Smith, breached the contract by failing to deliver the software module on time (E1).
- The Plaintiff's own actions and delays, including the late changes to the requirements (E2) and the delay in granting database access (E4), contributed to the overall delays and costs incurred.
- The Plaintiff incurred $35,000 in rework costs, as evidenced by the invoice from Acme's contractor (E6).
- The internal timeline (E7) shows that integration did not start until 2025-03-01, which was a result of the Defendant's failure to deliver the module on time.

=== CONCLUSIONS OF LAW ===
- The Defendant's breach of contract was the primary cause of the delays and costs incurred by the Plaintiff.
- The Plaintiff's own actions and delays contributed to the overall delays and costs incurred, but to a lesser extent than the Defendant's breach.
- A proportionate allocation of the delays and costs incurred is the most fair and reasonable approach.

=== ORDER / REMEDY ===
The Defendant, Jordan Smith, is ordered to pay the Plaintiff, Acme Corp., $40,000 in damages within 30 days of this order.

=== FULL WRITTEN OPINION ===
The Court's decision is based on the evidence presented and the arguments made by both parties. The Defendant's breach of contract was the primary cause of the delays and costs incurred by the Plaintiff. However, the Plaintiff's own actions and delays also contributed to the overall delays and costs incurred. A proportionate allocation of the delays and costs incurred is the most fair and reasonable approach. The Court's award of $40,000 in damages reflects the Defendant's breach of contract and the resulting delays and costs incurred by the Plaintiff, while also taking into account the Plaintiff's own contributions to the delays and costs.
