=== VERDICT ===
Based on the evidence presented and the arguments made by both parties, the court finds in favor of the Defendant, Jordan Smith.

=== FINDINGS OF FACT ===
- The Plaintiff, Acme Corp., unilaterally changed the requirements of the software module on January 10, 2025 (E2).
- The Defendant, Jordan Smith, requested database access by January 15, 2025, to stay on schedule, but access was not granted until February 5, 2025 (E4).
- The Plaintiff's actions prevented the Defendant from delivering the software module by the original deadline.

=== CONCLUSIONS OF LAW ===
- The Plaintiff's unilateral changes to the requirements and denial of necessary database access constituted a breach of the implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing.
- The Defendant's inability to deliver the software module by the original deadline was caused by the Plaintiff's actions, and therefore, the Defendant is not liable for damages.

=== ORDER / REMEDY ===
- The Plaintiff's claim for $50,000 in damages is dismissed.
- The Defendant's request for dismissal or reduction is granted.

=== FULL WRITTEN OPINION ===
The court's decision is based on the analysis of the evidence and the application of relevant legal principles. The Plaintiff's actions were found to be the primary cause of the Defendant's inability to deliver the software module by the original deadline. The Defendant's arguments, supported by evidence and legal precedents, demonstrated that the Plaintiff's breach of the implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing excused the Defendant's performance.
