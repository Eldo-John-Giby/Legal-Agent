=== VERDICT ===
Based on the evidence presented, I find in favor of the Plaintiff, Acme Corp. The Defendant, Jordan Smith, has failed to deliver the software module on time, and the Plaintiff has incurred damages as a result.

=== FINDINGS OF FACT ===
The court finds that the Defendant was responsible for delivering the software module by 2025-01-31, as stated in the Statement of Work (E1). The Plaintiff requested changes to the original agreement, which were communicated to the Defendant via Email E2. The Defendant requested access to the database, which was granted on 2025-02-05, as shown in the Ticket Log Excerpt (E4).

=== CONCLUSIONS OF LAW ===
The court concludes that the Defendant's failure to deliver the software module on time constitutes a breach of contract. The Plaintiff's changes to the original agreement did not excuse the Defendant's obligation to deliver the module on time. The Defendant's claim of impossibility/prevention is not supported by the evidence, as they failed to provide adequate notice of any issues that may have prevented them from delivering the module on time.

=== ORDER / REMEDY ===
The court orders the Defendant to pay the Plaintiff $50,000 in damages, as claimed. The Defendant is also responsible for court costs.

=== FULL WRITTEN OPINION ===
The court's decision is based on the evidence presented and the applicable law. The Defendant's breach of contract and failure to deliver the software module on time have caused damages to the Plaintiff. The Plaintiff's changes to the original agreement did not excuse the Defendant's obligation to deliver the module on time. The Defendant's claim of impossibility/prevention is not supported by the evidence.
