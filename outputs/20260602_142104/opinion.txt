=== VERDICT ===
The Defendant, Jordan Smith, is liable for damages in the amount of $30,000, as a result of breaching the contract by failing to deliver the software module on time.

=== FINDINGS OF FACT ===
- The Plaintiff and Defendant agreed to a contract with a deadline for delivery of Module X by 2025-01-31 (E1).
- The Plaintiff changed the requirements late in the project, adding additional reporting fields (E2).
- The Defendant agreed to include the additional reporting fields in the same delivery (E3).
- The Plaintiff delayed in granting access to the database, which was granted on 2025-02-05 (E4).
- The parties agreed to a revised scope (E5).
- The Plaintiff incurred rework costs of $35,000, as evidenced by the invoice from Acme's contractor (E6).
- The Plaintiff's internal timeline shows that integration started on 2025-03-01 (E7).

=== CONCLUSIONS OF LAW ===
- The Defendant breached the contract by failing to deliver the software module on time, resulting in damages to the Plaintiff.
- The Plaintiff's late change in requirements and delay in granting access to the database were contributing factors to the damages claimed, but the Defendant's failure to manage the project timeline and communicate effectively with the Plaintiff was also a significant factor.

=== ORDER / REMEDY ===
The Defendant, Jordan Smith, is ordered to pay the Plaintiff, Acme Corp., $30,000 in damages, which reflects the harm suffered by the Plaintiff due to the Defendant's breach of contract.

=== FULL WRITTEN OPINION ===
The court has carefully considered the evidence and arguments presented by both parties. The contract between the Plaintiff and Defendant clearly outlined the deadline for delivery of Module X, and the Defendant's failure to meet this deadline resulted in damages to the Plaintiff. However, the court also finds that the Plaintiff's late change in requirements and delay in granting access to the database were significant contributing factors to the damages claimed. The court has taken these factors into account in determining the amount of damages to be awarded.
