TITLE: State of Maharashtra v. Rajesh Verma
JURISDICTION: India – Criminal Trial (Indian Penal Code, 1860 / Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita equivalent may be assumed)

PARTIES:

PROSECUTION: State of Maharashtra
ACCUSED: Rajesh Verma

CHARGES (PROSECUTION):

Criminal breach of trust by employee.
Misappropriation of company funds amounting to ₹12,50,000.
Falsification of financial records to conceal unauthorized transfers.

DEFENSES (ACCUSED):

Authorized transactions: Transfers were approved verbally by senior management.
Lack of intent: No dishonest intention to cause wrongful loss.
Investigation defects: Police failed to collect complete accounting records and ignored exculpatory evidence.

FACTS:

Rajesh Verma worked as Finance Manager at Zenith Logistics Pvt. Ltd.
Between January and April 2024, multiple transfers totaling ₹12,50,000 were made from the company account to a consulting firm called "RV Business Solutions."
The prosecution alleges that RV Business Solutions was secretly controlled by Rajesh Verma.
An internal audit discovered the transfers and filed a police complaint.
Rajesh claims the consulting firm provided legitimate business services and that management was aware of the payments.

EVIDENCE:

E1: Employment Contract

Rajesh Verma appointed Finance Manager with authority to process payments up to ₹5,00,000 subject to documentation requirements.

E2: Bank Statement

Shows five transfers totaling ₹12,50,000 from Zenith Logistics Pvt. Ltd. to RV Business Solutions between 2024-01-15 and 2024-04-20.

E3: Internal Audit Report

Concludes no invoices, work orders, or service agreements exist for payments made to RV Business Solutions.

E4: Registrar of Companies / Business Registration Records

Lists Rajesh Verma's spouse as proprietor of RV Business Solutions.

E5: Email dated 2024-02-03

Rajesh to Accounts Team: "Process payment urgently. Management approval already obtained."

E6: Testimony of Managing Director

States no approval was granted for payments to RV Business Solutions and that he first learned of the firm during the audit.

E7: WhatsApp Chat dated 2024-01-14

Managing Director to Rajesh: "We need outside help for vendor optimization. Please handle it."
Defense argues this message demonstrates authorization to engage consultants.

E8: Tax Invoice

Submitted by defense, showing consultancy services worth ₹4,00,000 allegedly provided by RV Business Solutions.

E9: Police Investigation Report

Records seizure of accounting records and statements from company employees.

ISSUES FOR DETERMINATION:

Whether Rajesh Verma had authority to make the disputed payments.
Whether RV Business Solutions provided genuine services to the company.
Whether the accused dishonestly misappropriated company funds.
Whether the prosecution has proved guilt beyond reasonable doubt.

REQUESTED RELIEF:

PROSECUTION:

Conviction of the accused.
Recovery of ₹12,50,000.
Appropriate sentence under applicable criminal law.

DEFENSE:

Acquittal of all charges.
Alternatively, benefit of doubt due to insufficient proof of dishonest intent.

POTENTIAL WITNESSES:

PW1: Managing Director of Zenith Logistics Pvt. Ltd.
PW2: Internal Auditor.
PW3: Investigating Officer.
DW1: Rajesh Verma (Accused).
DW2: Proprietor of RV Business Solutions.
DW3: Accounts Executive who processed the transfers.

The court has carefully considered the arguments and evidence presented by both the Plaintiff and the Defendant. The case of State of Maharashtra v. Rajesh Verma revolves around allegations of criminal breach of trust, misappropriation of company funds, and falsification of financial records.

## Analysis of Evidence
The Plaintiff has presented evidence, including the Bank Statement (E2) and the Internal Audit Report (E3), which suggests that the transactions in question were unauthorized and lacked proper documentation. The Managing Director's testimony (E6) also states that no approval was granted for these payments. However, the Defendant has introduced the WhatsApp chat (E7) as evidence of implicit approval from the Managing Director, as well as the Tax Invoice (E8) to support the claim that RV Business Solutions provided genuine consultancy services.

## Application of Legal Precedents
The court has considered the legal precedents cited by both parties, including the case of Ramchand vs. Harijan Sevak Sangh and The Managing Director (Mig) Hindustan vs. Ajit Prasad Tarway. These precedents emphasize the importance of explicit approval, the consideration of all circumstances and evidence, and the principles of natural justice and fair trial.

## Conclusion
After careful consideration of the evidence and legal precedents, the court finds that the Plaintiff has not proved the allegations beyond a reasonable doubt. The evidence presented by the Defendant, including the WhatsApp chat and the Tax Invoice, raises sufficient doubts about the unauthorized nature of the transactions and the absence of implicit approval.

## Final Verdict
Based on the analysis of the evidence and the application of legal precedents, the court delivers a verdict of not guilty. The accused, Rajesh Verma, is hereby acquitted of all charges.
