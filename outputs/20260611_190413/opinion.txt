## Introduction to Final Opinion
The court has carefully considered the arguments and evidence presented by both the Plaintiff and the Defendant. The case of State of Maharashtra v. Rajesh Verma revolves around allegations of criminal breach of trust, misappropriation of company funds, and falsification of financial records.

## Analysis of Evidence
The Plaintiff has presented evidence, including the Bank Statement (E2) and the Internal Audit Report (E3), which suggests that the transactions in question were unauthorized and lacked proper documentation. The Managing Director's testimony (E6) also states that no approval was granted for these payments. However, the Defendant has introduced the WhatsApp chat (E7) as evidence of implicit approval from the Managing Director, as well as the Tax Invoice (E8) to support the claim that RV Business Solutions provided genuine consultancy services.

## Application of Legal Precedents
The court has considered the legal precedents cited by both parties, including the case of Ramchand vs. Harijan Sevak Sangh and The Managing Director (Mig) Hindustan vs. Ajit Prasad Tarway. These precedents emphasize the importance of explicit approval, the consideration of all circumstances and evidence, and the principles of natural justice and fair trial.

## Conclusion
After careful consideration of the evidence and legal precedents, the court finds that the Plaintiff has not proved the allegations beyond a reasonable doubt. The evidence presented by the Defendant, including the WhatsApp chat and the Tax Invoice, raises sufficient doubts about the unauthorized nature of the transactions and the absence of implicit approval.

## Final Verdict
Based on the analysis of the evidence and the application of legal precedents, the court delivers a verdict of not guilty. The accused, Rajesh Verma, is hereby acquitted of all charges.
