=== VERDICT ===
The Plaintiff, Acme Corp., wins on the claim of breach of contract, but with reduced damages.

=== FINDINGS OF FACT ===
- The Defendant, Jordan Smith, failed to deliver Module X by the deadline specified in the "Statement of Work" (E1).
- The Plaintiff, Acme Corp., requested additional reporting fields on 2025-01-10 (E2), which the Defendant acknowledged as feasible on 2025-01-12 (E3).
- The Plaintiff delayed in granting access to the Defendant, which was ultimately provided on 2025-02-05 (E4).
- The parties agreed to a revised scope on 2025-02-06 (E5), but the Defendant still failed to deliver the module on time.
- The Plaintiff incurred rework costs of $35,000 (E6) and experienced delays in the integration timeline, which started on 2025-03-01 (E7).

=== CONCLUSIONS OF LAW ===
- The Defendant, Jordan Smith, breached the contract by failing to deliver Module X by the deadline.
- The Plaintiff, Acme Corp., contributed to the delays and damages incurred, including the request for additional reporting fields and the delay in granting access.
- The Defendant's breach was a significant contributing factor to the damages incurred by the Plaintiff, but not the sole cause.

=== ORDER / REMEDY ===
The court awards damages in the amount of $30,000 to the Plaintiff, Acme Corp., which reflects the Defendant's significant contribution to the breach and the Plaintiff's contribution to the delays and damages incurred.

=== FULL WRITTEN OPINION ===
The court's decision is based on the evidence presented and the applicable law. The Defendant's failure to deliver Module X by the deadline was a breach of contract, but the Plaintiff's contributions to the delays and damages incurred must also be considered. The court exercises its discretion to reduce the damages from the Plaintiff's requested amount of $50,000, considering the mutual contributions to the delays and damages. This outcome reflects a balanced approach, acknowledging the Defendant's breach while also accounting for the Plaintiff's role in the delays and damages.
