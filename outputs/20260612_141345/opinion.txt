=== VERDICT ===
Based on the evidence presented, it appears that the Defendant was faced with unforeseen changes and access restrictions that impacted their ability to deliver the software module on time. However, the Plaintiff's insistence on maintaining the original deadline despite these changes also played a role in the delays.

=== FINDINGS OF FACT ===
1. The Plaintiff and Defendant entered into a contract for the delivery of a software module by 2025-01-31.
2. The Plaintiff requested changes to the scope, including additional reporting fields, which the Defendant agreed to incorporate.
3. The Defendant requested database access by 2025-01-15 to meet the deadline, but access was not granted until 2025-02-05.
4. The parties agreed to a revised scope, but the Plaintiff maintained that the original deadline should still be met.

=== CONCLUSIONS OF LAW ===
Given the mutual changes and the unforeseen delays, it is reasonable to conclude that both parties contributed to the failure to meet the deadline. The Plaintiff's changes and denial of timely access, combined with their expectation to still meet the original deadline, suggest that the breach of contract, if any, was not solely the Defendant's responsibility.

=== ORDER / REMEDY ===
Considering the shared responsibility and the damages incurred, I order that the Plaintiff is entitled to a reduced amount of damages, reflecting the proportion of responsibility that can be attributed to the Defendant's actions.

=== FULL WRITTEN OPINION ===
The intricacies of this case highlight the importance of clear communication, timely access, and flexibility in contract negotiations. Both parties must recognize their roles in the delays and work towards a more cooperative approach in future engagements.
