=== VERDICT ===

The Defendant is found to be in breach of contract, and the Plaintiff is awarded $50,000 in damages.

=== FINDINGS OF FACT ===

The court finds that the Defendant failed to deliver the software module on time, despite agreeing to incorporate the additional reporting fields into the delivery. The court also finds that the delay in granting access was not solely responsible for the failure to meet the deadline.

=== CONCLUSIONS OF LAW ===

The court concludes that the Defendant's claim of impossibility and frustration of contract is not supported by the evidence. The court also concludes that the Plaintiff's claim of $50,000 in damages is reasonable and supported by the evidence.

=== ORDER / REMEDY ===

The Defendant is ordered to pay $50,000 in damages to the Plaintiff.

=== FULL WRITTEN OPINION ===

The court's decision is based on the evidence and arguments presented. The Defendant's failure to deliver the software module on time, despite agreeing to incorporate the additional reporting fields into the delivery, constitutes a breach of contract. The delay in granting access was not solely responsible for the failure to meet the deadline, and the Defendant's claim of impossibility and frustration of contract is not supported by the evidence.

The Plaintiff's claim of $50,000 in damages is reasonable and supported by the evidence. The Defendant's breach of contract has caused significant harm to the Plaintiff, and the damages incurred are a direct result of the Defendant's actions.

In conclusion, the court finds in favor of the Plaintiff and awards $50,000 in damages. The Defendant is ordered to pay the damages to the Plaintiff.
