=== VERDICT ===
The court finds in favor of the Plaintiff, Acme Corp. The Defendant, Jordan Smith, is liable for breach of contract and damages incurred due to the late delivery of the software module.

=== FINDINGS OF FACT ===
- The Defendant failed to deliver the software module by the agreed-upon deadline of 2025-01-31 (E1).
- The Plaintiff requested additional reporting fields on 2025-01-10 (E2), which the Defendant agreed to incorporate into the same delivery.
- The Defendant requested database access by 2025-01-15 to stay on schedule (E3), but access was not granted until 2025-02-05 (E4).
- The parties agreed to a revised scope on 2025-02-06 (E5), but the Defendant still failed to meet the original deadline.
- The Plaintiff incurred significant rework and delay costs due to the Defendant's breach, as evidenced by the invoice from Acme contractor (E6) and the internal Acme timeline (E7).

=== CONCLUSIONS OF LAW ===
- The Defendant's failure to deliver the software module by the deadline constitutes a breach of contract.
- The Plaintiff's request for additional reporting fields and delayed granting of database access contributed to the delay, but the Defendant's own actions and failure to adapt to changing requirements were the primary causes of the breach.
- The concept of impossibility and prevention does not apply in this case, as the Defendant had agreed to incorporate the additional reporting fields into the same delivery and had not demonstrated that the delay was impossible to overcome.

=== ORDER / REMEDY ===
The court orders the Defendant, Jordan Smith, to pay the Plaintiff, Acme Corp., damages in the amount of $50,000, representing the rework and delay costs incurred due to the Defendant's breach of contract.

=== FULL WRITTEN OPINION ===
The court's decision is based on the evidence presented and the applicable law. The Defendant's breach of contract and failure to deliver the software module by the deadline have caused significant harm to the Plaintiff. The court has considered the principle of impossibility and prevention, as well as the concept of contributory negligence, and has determined that the Defendant is liable for the damages incurred by the Plaintiff.
