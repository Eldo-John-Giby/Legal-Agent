Thank you, Your Honor. The defense would like to reiterate that the prosecution's case relies heavily on circumstantial evidence and lacks concrete proof of the accused's intent to commit a crime. We have consistently argued that the accused acted in good faith and within the scope of his authority as Finance Manager of Zenith Logistics Pvt. Ltd.

The WhatsApp chat dated 2024-01-14 and the Email dated 2024-02-03 (E5) demonstrate that the accused had obtained management approval for the payments to RV Business Solutions. The prosecution's attempt to discredit this evidence by suggesting that it is incomplete or misleading is unfounded.

Furthermore, the defense has highlighted the inconsistencies in the prosecution's narrative, particularly with regard to the Managing Director's testimony (E6). The Managing Director's claim that no approval was granted for the specific payments to RV Business Solutions is contradicted by the WhatsApp chat and the Email dated 2024-02-03 (E5).

The defense also takes issue with the prosecution's characterization of the Tax Invoice (E8) as a fabricated document. We argue that this invoice is a genuine record of the consultancy services provided by RV Business Solutions, and it is unfair to dismiss it as a fabrication without credible evidence.

Throughout this trial, the prosecution has attempted to portray the accused as a dishonest and deceitful individual. However, the defense believes that this portrayal is unfair and not supported by the evidence. The accused has consistently maintained that he acted in good faith and with the intention of benefiting the company.

In conclusion, the defense urges the court to consider the doubts and uncertainties surrounding the prosecution's case. We request that the court acquit the accused of the alleged offenses of criminal breach of trust, misappropriation of company funds, and falsification of financial records. ### Judgment

After careful consideration of the evidence and arguments presented, the court finds the accused, Rajesh Verma, guilty of criminal breach of trust and misappropriation of company funds. The prosecution has established that the accused made unauthorized transfers of ₹12,50,000 to RV Business Solutions, a firm secretly controlled by the accused, and that these transactions were not authorized by the management of Zenith Logistics Pvt. Ltd.

The court also finds that the accused falsified financial records to conceal these unauthorized transfers, which demonstrates a clear intent to deceive and mislead. The defense's arguments regarding the authorization of the transactions are unfounded, and the evidence presented by the prosecution establishes the accused's guilt beyond reasonable doubt.

The court hereby sentences the accused, Rajesh Verma, to imprisonment for a period of three years and orders the recovery of ₹12,50,000 from the accused.

The court's decision is based on a thorough examination of the evidence and arguments presented. The prosecution's case has established that the accused committed serious offenses, including criminal breach of trust and misappropriation of company funds. The court's sentence reflects the severity of the offenses committed by the accused and the need to protect the integrity of the company's financial management system.
