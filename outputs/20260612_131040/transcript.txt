=== VERDICT ===

After careful consideration of the evidence presented and the arguments made by both parties, the court finds in favor of the Plaintiff, Acme Corp. The court determines that the Defendant, Jordan Smith, breached the contract by failing to deliver the software module on time, resulting in damages to the Plaintiff.

The court's decision is based on the evidence presented, including the Statement of Work (E1), the email dated 2025-01-10 from the Acme Project Manager (E2), and the Internal Acme Timeline (E7). The court finds that the Plaintiff's request for changes to the reporting fields on January 10, 2025, was a necessary adjustment to ensure the software module met the required specifications, and that the Defendant had sufficient time to incorporate the changes.

The court also considers the Defendant's argument that the Plaintiff's changes to the requirements and denial of needed access made it impossible to meet the deadline. However, the court finds that the Defendant's request for database access on January 12, 2025 (E3), and the delay in granting access until February 5, 2025 (E4), do not entirely excuse the Defendant's failure to deliver the software module on time.

=== DAMAGES ===

The court awards the Plaintiff $50,000 in damages, as requested, to cover the rework and delay costs incurred due to the Defendant's breach of contract.

=== CONCLUSION ===

In conclusion, the court finds that the Defendant, Jordan Smith, breached the contract with the Plaintiff, Acme Corp., and awards damages in the amount of $50,000.
