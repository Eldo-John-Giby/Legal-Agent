TITLE: State of X v. Doe
JURISDICTION: Constitutional Mock Trial

PARTIES:
- PLAINTIFF: John Doe (Citizen)
- DEFENDANT: State of X (Government)

CLAIMS (PLAINTIFF):
- Violation of Right to Equality: The State's new recruitment policy discriminates based on place of birth.
- Violation of Fundamental Rights: The policy is inconsistent with Part III of the Constitution.

DEFENSES (DEFENDANT):
- Reasonable Classification: The policy is intended for the advancement of backward classes.
- State Power: The State has the power to make special provisions for certain regions.

EVIDENCE:
E1: Government Order #123: "Only residents of District A are eligible for recruitment in Department B."
E2: Doe's Birth Certificate: Shows place of birth as District C.
E3: Doe's Application Rejection: "Rejected due to non-residency in District A."
E4: State Statistical Report: Shows District A has the lowest employment rate in the state.

REQUESTED RELIEF:
- Plaintiff asks to strike down the policy as unconstitutional.
- Defendant asks to uphold the policy as a valid special provision.
